Inside the Store

Located on the basement floor of Cuppage Plaza, the restaurant has table seating, a counter and a raised area, making it suitable for banquets of around 12 people, small drinking parties or everyday dining.

Introducing the Proprietress

Seino Hatsuho

Originally from Sapporo, Hokkaido, she trained in Japanese cuisine in Japan before moving to Singapore, where she has lived for 23 years.

She previously worked as the proprietress of a long-established izakaya until the demolition of Hotel Grand Central. In September 2012, she opened Sakana Keria.

“My grandparents used to run an izakaya in Hokkaido, and the name of the restaurant, ‘Yamabuki,’ comes from the English word ‘Kerria.’ We sincerely hope to continue receiving your kind patronage.”

— Hatsuho Seino
